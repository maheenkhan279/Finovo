How to Use FINOVO

Learn how to play and master our financial education games

What is FINOVO?

FINOVO is an interactive financial literacy platform that teaches personal finance through engaging, game-based learning. Our platform offers three progressive game levels designed to build your financial knowledge from the ground up.

Whether you're a complete beginner or looking to enhance your financial skills, FINOVO provides a safe, risk-free environment to practice financial decision-making and learn essential money management concepts.

Getting Started

  1. Create an Account: Sign up for a free FINOVO account using your email and a secure password.
  2. Login: Access your account and navigate to the Financial Games section.
  3. Choose Your Level: Start with the Beginner level game, then progress to Moderate and Advanced as you master each concept.
  4. Track Progress: Monitor your scores and completion rates to see your financial literacy improve over time.
1

Beginner Level: Needs vs Wants Quiz

Perfect for Financial Beginners

How to Play:

  1. Click "Play Game" to start the Needs vs Wants quiz.
  2. Read each scenario carefully and determine if it's a Need (essential) or Want (optional).
  3. Select your answer and click "Submit".
  4. Review the explanation to understand why each item is classified as a need or want.
  5. Complete all questions to see your final score and progress to the next level.
Learning Goal:

Master the fundamental skill of distinguishing between essential expenses (needs) and discretionary spending (wants), which is crucial for effective budgeting.

2

Moderate Level: Budget Balancer Game

Build Practical Budgeting Skills

How to Play:

  1. Start the Budget Balancer game from the Games page.
  2. You'll receive a monthly income and a list of expenses.
  3. Allocate your income across different expense categories (Housing, Food, Transportation, etc.).
  4. Prioritize essential expenses and try to save at least 20% of your income.
  5. Balance your budget across multiple months while handling unexpected events.
  6. Monitor your remaining balance - it should never go negative!
  7. Click "Next Month" to progress and "Reset" to start over if needed.
Learning Goal:

Learn to create and maintain a balanced budget, prioritize expenses, build emergency savings, and make financial decisions that support long-term financial health.

3

Advanced Level: Stock Market Mini Simulator

Master Investment Concepts

How to Play:

  1. Launch the Stock Market Mini Simulator from the Games page.
  2. You start with a virtual cash balance (e.g., $10,000) to invest.
  3. Browse available stocks and their current prices.
  4. Click "Buy" to purchase stocks - enter the quantity you want to buy.
  5. Monitor your portfolio value as stock prices change.
  6. Click "Sell" to sell stocks from your portfolio when you want to realize gains or cut losses.
  7. Track your profit/loss and total portfolio value.
  8. Use "Next Day" to advance time and see how market changes affect your investments.
  9. Click "Reset" to start a new simulation with fresh capital.
Learning Goal:

Understand stock market basics, learn about buying and selling stocks, experience market volatility, practice portfolio management, and develop risk awareness - all without risking real money.

Tips for Success

  • Start at Beginner: Even if you have some financial knowledge, the beginner game reinforces fundamental concepts.
  • Read Explanations: Pay attention to feedback and explanations - they teach valuable financial principles.
  • Practice Regularly: Replay games to improve your scores and deepen your understanding.
  • Learn from Mistakes: Don't be afraid to make errors - that's how you learn in a risk-free environment.
  • Progress Gradually: Master each level before moving to the next for the best learning experience.
